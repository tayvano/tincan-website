TinCan is a messaging app with one key difference: TinCan never connects to the internet.

When you post a message on TinCan, that message must hop from your mobile device to another mobile device to spread. When two devices with TinCan are close to each other, they exchange messages that they don’t have in common.

Obviously, TinCan is not an efficient global messaging platform! TinCan has no central database, and no one can see every TinCan message that is out there. If you post a TinCan message in Denver, it can’t be read by someone in Dubai until a mobile device with TinCan physically carries it there.

However, in some ways TinCan is special. TinCan is not affected where access to the internet is unavailable or has been disrupted.
